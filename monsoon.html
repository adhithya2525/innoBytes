𝖬𝗈o𝗌𝗈𝗈𝗇 𝖥𝗈𝗈𝖽𝗌 is a comforting and flavorful part of the rainy season in many cultures. During the monsoon, people crave hot, spicy, and crispy dishes that warm the body and satisfy cravings. Popular monsoon foods include fried snacks like pakoras (vegetable fritters), samosas, and bhajis, which pair perfectly with a hot cup of tea. Soups, stews, and curries made with seasonal vegetables are also favorites, as they provide warmth and nourishment. These dishes often feature spices like ginger, garlic, and chili, which are believed to help boost immunity during the damp, chilly weather.





𝘚𝘰𝘮𝘦 𝘱𝘰𝘱𝘶𝘭𝘢𝘳 𝘮𝘰𝘯𝘴𝘰𝘰𝘯 𝘧𝘰𝘰𝘥𝘴 𝘪𝘯𝘤𝘭𝘶𝘥𝘦:

Pakoras – Deep-fried fritters made from gram flour, often stuffed with vegetables like                    potatoes, spinach, or onions, served with chutney.
Samosas – Crispy pastry pockets filled with spiced potatoes, peas, or meat, perfect for                    rainy days.
Bhajis – Similar to pakoras, these are deep-fried snacks made from various vegetables                    like onions, spinach, or eggplant.
Chai (Tea) – A hot, spiced tea made with ginger, cardamom, and other spices, often                          enjoyed with snacks like pakoras or biscuits.
Corn on the Cob – Grilled or boiled corn, seasoned with salt, butter, or chili powder, is                    a popular monsoon snack.
Pav Bhaji – A spicy mashed vegetable curry served with buttered, toasted bread rolls,                      offering warmth and comfort.
Methi Thepla – A flatbread made from fenugreek leaves, perfect to pair with yogurt or                    pickles.
Khichdi – A soothing, one-pot dish made with rice, lentils, and spices, often enjoyed                        with a side of yogurt or pickle.
Aloo Tikki – Spiced potato patties, often served with tamarind or mint chutney.
Soup – Hot, hearty soups made from seasonal vegetables like tomatoes, pumpkin, or                         lentils, providing warmth during the rainy season.
